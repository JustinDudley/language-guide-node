So you want to choose a programming language...

So many choices! How to pick? As a bootcamp graduate, now in my third year in the industry, I have learned a few languages and made a few mistakes. This guide is designed for people who are coming to programming from a different job or career, and do not necessarily have the support, guidance, and structure of a university program. It is meant for people who are looking to be full-time software developers, not so much for those already in a science-heavy industry who want to make themselves more valuable by picking up a language to help with their current position.

There are many languages, but to pick a first language, there are really only, in my opinion, three, maybe four good options. This is a good thing! Your job at the beginning is simple.

    Here are some things this site WON'T tell you:
  • Salaries associated with a language. Junior developers make a similar salary regardless of language. After you've spent a few years in the industry, you'll be able to target certain positions, salary ranges, and languages.
  • Percentages of software developers using a given language. This information can be useful, but it is also misleading to junior developers, since certain languages like Swift are used primarily by senior devs. (Skew effect)
  • 25 fascinating programming languages, none of which are a common entry point into the industry.

Also, I should note that any language you choose will lead you in a good direction. All languages share certain features, such as functions and fdjfkdj and fjdkjf, and once you are proficient in one, it is not difficult to learn another. It is not uncommon to try a couple languages in the first year and flail around a bit. Try to settle into one language as soon as you can and get good at it. Certain tooling, such as git and gitHub, will also have to be learned. By the way, learning a new language is a big project.

Finally, learning a computer language throughly takes many years. You don't need to learn everything at once. If you enjoy programming, and enjoy the industry, you will have a long time to get better and better.
