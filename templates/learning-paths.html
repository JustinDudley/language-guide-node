My story:

It took me about one year to learn enough to get hired. I started with a bunch of random python tutorials, then did a 6-month part-time online bootcamp learning python and java, all while still working another full-time job. Finally I bit the bullet and did a 3-month in-person bootcamp, learning C#, javascript, and Angular.

My advice:
  • You can start with zero-depth-entry tutorials like codacademy which spoon-feed you coding syntax without your having to install anything on your laptop.
  • Pretty soon you'll want to learn how to code on your own, and you'll begin to learn how to set up your own machine to do that.
  • Especially at the beginning, always use tutorials that are recent. Anything more than a year old is bound to have information that is out of date. There are work-arounds for those situations, but when you are starting out, it is very difficult to problem-solve those. Don't be afraid to spend $18 on a tutorial.
  • Free stuff is great, especially at the very beginning, but don't be afraid to drop $18 on a course here and there. When you pay money, you are more likly to get material that is (1) high-quality and (2) up-to-date.
