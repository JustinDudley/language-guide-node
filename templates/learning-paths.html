-- Also, I should include a section that gives tips on HOW to learn: Try codeacademy first because it is a zero-entry environment. Then quickly switch to a tutorial that requires you to install some things and work in your own environment. Always use a tutorial that is recent (less than a year old), since anything older will have pieces that you have to problem-solve, and that may be beyond your capability at the beginning. Don't be afraid to spend $18 on a tutorial. Free stuff is great, but a money-course has a better chance of being up-to-date and really high-quality. (My current favorit is Udemy, but you will find your own path). I COULD tell them MY path: Online tutorials, a 6-month part-time online bootcamp while I was still in my former career, then a full-on 3-month bootcamp, then a LOT of learning on-the-job after I landed my first job.
